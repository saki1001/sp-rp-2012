A. Haag Sherman

Co-Founder

A. Haag Sherman is a Co-Founder of Salient. Mr. Sherman has been responsible for developing most of Salient's investment programs. Mr. Sherman also developed the intermediary services plan for Salient's investment programs and previously served as Chief Investment Officer.

Previously, Mr. Sherman was a Partner and Executive Officer of Redstone where he focused on private equity investments and alternative investments from 1998 to 2002. From 1996 to 1998, Mr. Sherman was employed at PaineWebber, Inc. as a part of its Redstone Consulting wealth advisory group. Mr. Sherman began his career with Price Waterhouse, and also practiced corporate law for Akin, Gump, Strauss, Hauer, & Feld LLP.

Mr. Sherman is an honors graduate of the University of Texas School of Law and a cum laude graduate of Baylor University. He is also a certified public accountant and a member of the State Bar of Texas.

He serves on the Advisory Board of Baylor University's Hankamer School of Business and the Board of Directors of PlainsCapital Bank (NYSE: PCB - to be listed in November 2009), where he serves as Chairman of the Compensation Committee and a member of the Audit Committee. He also serves on the Associate Vestry of St. John's the Divine Episcopal Church in Houston.

Mr. Sherman is author of Shattering Orthodoxies: An Economic and Foreign Policy Blueprint for America. It outlines the causes of the current economic crisis and provides an integrated plan to address the crisis. The book reached no. 6 among Social Policy books on Amazon.com.

Mr. Sherman is married to Millette Lewis Sherman and has two young daughters.
